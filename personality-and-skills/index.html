Personality and skills

Big Five is a personality frame considered universal. Among organizational psychologists, it is a consensus in terms of effectiveness of assessment. Based on it, several studies and meta-studies were sought, focusing especially on general potential, leadership potential, psychometric methods, selection and others specific to each attribute or group of attributes (such as cognitive skills, interpersonal skills, need for achievement, among others) in order to seek a nomenclature that made more sense, thus creating a mixed frame of personality and skill. (Excerpt of Mindmatch report.)

Conceptual thinking

Formulating strategies and giving visibility to your work and company for the team and other stakeholders.

Reflection

Accelerating learning and offer insights to your position and sector.

Creative thinking

Developing innovating solutions and thinking about new ways to solve problems.

Ambition

Always seeks excellence in the projects and only having the best as reference.

Consideration for the others

Helping people in difficult moments and guaranteeing team satisfaction with the work environment.

Analytical capacity

Analyzing root-causes of problems and manipulating numbers and diverse information.

Assertiveness

Exposing opinions that other people end up avoiding and increasing communication efficiency.

Risk taking

Acting against the status quo in order to ensure that the organization does not settle.

Flexibility

Performing faster after changes and being able to be reallocated with greater ease.

Initiative

Acting even when the direction is not clear and having a higher execution output.

Communication

Explaining difficult concepts to the group and clearly transmit ideas to several people.

Planning and organization

Formulating and implementing processes and performing with excellence in the work flow.

Influence

Selling ideas to internal and external clients and conquering resources for the execution of the projects.

Emotional stability

Maintaining the team focused during turbulences and keeping the work going even with many external interferences.

Facilitation

Solving organizational conflicts and ensuring teamwork in a team.

Sociability

Creating networking with stakeholders and connecting people.
